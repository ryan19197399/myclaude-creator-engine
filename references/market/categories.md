# Marketplace Categories

The 9 product categories on MyClaude marketplace. Each category description, typical
pricing, and demand signals based on framework analysis.

---

## Skills

**What buyers expect:** A callable routine that transforms their Claude Code session.
Skills are the most demanded category — they directly extend what Claude Code can do.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Domain-specific analysis (security, performance, architecture review)
- Systematic methodologies (reasoning frameworks, decision analysis)
- Content transformation (writing, documentation, summarization with structure)
- Code review and quality gates

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free — $9
- MCS-2: $9 — $29
- MCS-3: $29 — $79

**Demand indicators:**
- Highest search volume of all categories
- Most downloads per published product
- Highest repeat purchase rate (creators buy skills from other creators)
- Strong category diversity — no single skill dominates

**Competition level:** High (most products published here). Stand out with:
- Domain specificity (security for Go APIs, not "security in general")
- Encoded methodology (your unique approach, not AI-generated instructions)

---

## Agents

**What buyers expect:** A specialized persona they can invoke as a co-worker or
subject matter expert, with consistent behavior across different tasks.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Specialist reviewers (security auditor, architecture reviewer, code reviewer)
- Domain experts (legal drafter, data analyst, UX researcher)
- Quality gates (validator, fact-checker, editor)
- Research assistants with specific cognitive models

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free — $15
- MCS-2: $15 — $49
- MCS-3: $49 — $99

**Demand indicators:**
- Fast-growing category — buyers increasingly want specialized personas over general use
- Strong demand for agents with THINKER cognitive model (reasoning-focused)
- High-value: buyers often purchase agents to replace specialized human consultants

**Competition level:** Medium. Poorly differentiated agents (generic personas) are
common, leaving room for truly specialized agents.

---

## Squads

**What buyers expect:** A team that handles complex, multi-stage work autonomously.
The value proposition: "I give it a brief and get a finished output."

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Content production pipelines (research → write → edit)
- Development workflows (plan → build → review → test)
- Research intelligence (search → synthesize → validate → report)
- Marketing automation (audit → strategize → create → review)

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: $15 — $39
- MCS-2: $39 — $99
- MCS-3: $99 — $249

**Demand indicators:**
- Lower volume but higher price per sale
- Buyers have high intent — they know what they need
- Strong brand opportunity — a well-marketed squad becomes the "go-to" for a workflow

**Competition level:** Low. High creation complexity means fewer products, more
opportunity for quality products to win.

---

## Workflows

**What buyers expect:** A repeatable process definition they can run to automate a
specific recurring task. More procedural than agent-based.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Release workflows (code review → testing → changelog → publish)
- Onboarding workflows (project setup → conventions → documentation)
- Research workflows (intake → search → synthesize → output)
- Content workflows (brief → outline → draft → review → publish)

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free — $9
- MCS-2: $9 — $29
- MCS-3: $29 — $59

**Demand indicators:**
- Medium demand — growing as Claude Code adoption increases
- Buyers value proven workflows from experienced practitioners
- Integration patterns matter: buyers want workflows that work with their existing tools

**Competition level:** Medium-low. Opportunity in domain-specific workflows.

## Design Systems

**What buyers expect:** Production-ready token sets and component specs they can
install and start building with immediately.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Dark-mode developer tool aesthetics
- SaaS dashboard design systems
- Marketing site design systems
- Brand kits with token exports

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free — $19
- MCS-2: $19 — $59
- MCS-3: $59 — $149

**Demand indicators:**
- Growing demand as Claude Code is increasingly used for UI projects
- Strong demand for OKLCH-based systems (modern color approach)
- Buyers value Tailwind + CSS variables + DTCG triple export

**Competition level:** Low. Few high-quality design systems published.
Opportunity exists for niche aesthetics (brutalist, glassmorphism, etc.).

## Prompts

**What buyers expect:** A tested, reliable prompt for a specific task they use
repeatedly. Often the entry product for new marketplace users.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- System prompts for specific professional roles
- Structured output generators (JSON, specific markdown formats)
- Context engineering templates
- Multi-variant writing prompts

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free
- MCS-2: Free — $9
- MCS-3: $9 — $29

**Demand indicators:**
- High volume, low price — prompts are the "quick win" category
- Good for building reputation before investing in higher-tier products
- Strong category for beginners

**Competition level:** Very high, but quality is generally low. A well-structured
MCS-3 prompt with real examples stands out significantly.

## CLAUDE.md Configurations

**What buyers expect:** A battle-tested CLAUDE.md for their tech stack that they
can install and immediately benefit from, without building from scratch.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Framework-specific configs (Next.js, Django, FastAPI, Rails)
- Domain-specific configs (enterprise TypeScript, data science Python)
- Security-focused configs
- Team convention packs

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free
- MCS-2: Free — $15
- MCS-3: $15 — $49

**Demand indicators:**
- Fast-growing category — every Claude Code project needs one
- Buyers frequently purchase to save setup time on new projects
- Strong demand for opinionated configs from recognized practitioners

**Competition level:** Low. Most developers build their own. Sharing yours is
a clear opportunity.

## Applications

**What buyers expect:** Working software they can install and run. Highest trust
requirement — buyers need to know it actually works.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Developer CLI utilities (linters, formatters, analysis tools)
- Automation scripts built with Claude Code
- Template applications demonstrating Claude Code integration patterns
- Reference implementations

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: Free — $19
- MCS-2: $19 — $59
- MCS-3: $59 — $199

**Demand indicators:**
- Highest potential price per product
- Highest scrutiny from buyers — reviews focus on "does it work?"
- Strong demand for CLI tools that extend Claude Code's direct capabilities

**Competition level:** Low (hardest to create). High potential for differentiation.

---

## Systems

**What buyers expect:** A complete, integrated solution for a specific domain or
workflow. The premium tier. Buyers are solving a real problem and willing to pay.

**Typical use cases on marketplace:**
- Domain intelligence systems (research, analysis, synthesis)
- Production workflow systems (content, code, marketing)
- Specialized assistant systems (security, legal, data science)
- Meta-tools (systems that create other systems)

**Typical pricing:**
- MCS-1: $29 — $69
- MCS-2: $69 — $199
- MCS-3: $199 — $499

**Demand indicators:**
- Lowest volume, highest price — buyers with serious problems
- Brand is critical — a known creator's system gets premium pricing
- Buyers often purchase to solve a specific business problem, not casual use

**Competition level:** Very low. Creating a quality system is the hardest task.
Highest potential for significant revenue.

## Category Opportunity Matrix

| Category | Volume | Price Ceiling | Competition | Creation Effort |
|----------|--------|--------------|-------------|----------------|
| Skills | High | Medium | High | Low-Medium |
| Agents | Medium | Medium-High | Medium | Medium |
| Squads | Low-Medium | High | Low | High |
| Workflows | Medium | Medium | Low-Medium | Medium |
| Design Systems | Low | Medium-High | Low | Medium |
| Prompts | High | Low | Very High | Low |
| CLAUDE.md | Medium | Medium | Low | Low-Medium |
| Applications | Low | High | Low | Very High |
| Systems | Low | Very High | Very Low | Very High |

**Best first product:** Skills (low effort, high demand) or CLAUDE.md (low effort, growing category).
**Best premium product:** Systems or Squads (high price potential, low competition).
**Best reputation-builder:** Skills at MCS-3 (visible quality, broad audience).
